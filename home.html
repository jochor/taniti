About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of electrical outlets are used there?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Can alcohol be purchased at any time?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

What is the legal drinking age in Taniti?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

Do the locals speak English?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

Are there any health clinics in case of emergency?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Is Taniti safe?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

When is the best time to visit?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency can I use in Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks allow currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
